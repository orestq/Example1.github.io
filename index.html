Along with its history, mazes are still a part of the modern world, with these structures and puzzles becoming places that visitors can enjoy. Being surrounded by green hedges and finding your way to the goal, you can experience these places for yourself in many places around the world.

Historically, the first recorded labyrinth was the Egyptian Labyrinth, described by the Greek writer Herodotus. The structure was said to be surrounded by a wall with 3,000 chambers both above and below ground. It is also said that the chambers below ground contained the tombs of kings. Another famous labyrinth is the Cretan Labyrinth. This structure is found in ancient Greek mythology, created by Daedulus, and containing the minotaur at its center.

The Ashcombe Maze, located in Shoreham, Australia, is a beautiful maze with a stunning visual appearance. The maze has soft edges made of over 1000 Cupressus macrocarpa, also known as the Monterey cypress. These plants are carefully trimmed three or four times a year. This is to continually maintain the amazing shapes and light green color of the maze.

The creation and structure of the maze makes it an enjoyable puzzle for visitors. Your goal is to travel through the two halves of the maze. The structure also contains an interesting center space. Here you might take a moment to relax from the first half of the maze and prepare yourself for the new puzzles in the second half.
